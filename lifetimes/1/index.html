Game Developer

(August 2012 - August 2014)

How it begin

The journey began when I finished my job as a research assistant. I wasn’t sure about what to do next. I didn’t want to have a normal job, fearing that it will drain the passion out of me. I looked back into my past, seeking the thing that I loved - Games.

The Journey

I started with the LibGdx, a Java game library meant for mobile and desktop. It was simple and Java was the language that I was comfortable back then. I didn’t really make any game for Libgdx at that time, just toying around with basic concepts.

I started playing with XNA soon after, as I wanted to move to desktop games instead of mobile. After playing with XNA for a month or two, I realized I really hate coding on windows.

The next api I chose was C++/SFML. To be honest, choosing this was one of the best decision I have made so far. To explain why, let me tell you the backstory.

Prior to that day, I had always believed that C++ is hard and have been avoiding it since I started programming. However, after I started my knowledge obsession, I had became quite fearless. With that fearless mentality, I decided to conquer this long fear of mine to “prove” my determination.

So why is it the best decision? Because of it, I started using vim a lot. Although I did use vim prior to that during my research, it was during the time that I use C++ that I really became very good at vim. I also learnt a few other stuffs, bash, git, to name a few. And most importantly, I conquered the fear.

After conquering the fear, I realized that I could learn many things if I put my heart to it.

I used C++ for at least one and a half years. Most of the games can be found in my github account. Some of the games are also listed in this blog or will be listed in the future. I also wrote a Lua Integration Tutorial.

I had also published a game on the android, a simple memory game. It was prototyped in C++ in a day and I really like the concept. After playtesting with my friends for about a month, I made the android version and released it on playstore. It might have been taken down by now though.

How it ended

After writing games for many months, participating in many ludum dare(s), I was lost. I couldn’t find an exit. I started looking for job that I could be myself or job that can allow me to make game at the side. After searching for a few months, I finally found one. I decided to end this life earlier than the 3 years I gave myself, beginning my next life.

All the game I made in this life can be found at my game-project page along with other games that I after that.
