Background

I begin my ‘hacking’ career at around 17, when I took up a diploma in IT in 2003. The initial motivation to learn computing was to be able to make games, which I eventually did in my first life. I did pretty well in the course and gave me an opportunity to pursuit a Degree(Computer Science) in NUS.

Although I didn’t take a specialized course to make game, the degree in Computer Science turned out to be better choice. I begin to become interested in Computer Science(CS) in general and began to dive into many areas in CS.

Just before I graduated, I had the intention to take my Ph.D in the same university. This led me to one of my professor and I began to work as a research assistant under him. However, as it turns out, I wasn’t really interested in research and I left.

But it turns out, it is only after I left that my lifes truely begins.

Thoughts on knowledge

I wasn’t really interested in learning things when I was young. As I grow older, I keep learning things as they are needed until one day, I started becoming obsessed with gaining knowledge and learning things.

Previously, I would take lessons and courses that I knew I can do well, or because my friends were in there. After my mind was ‘opened’, I started taking courses that were interesting to me and even though they seems hard, I didn’t care.

I have my theory on this, which I will eventually write in a separate page. The sumamry of my theory is that, the more you know, the more you can know. The realization that there are more things that I can know drives me to learn more, and eventually even more.

I eventually became obsessed with ways to keep the knowledge that I know, fearing that I will lose them eventually. This blog is just another manifestation of that fear.
